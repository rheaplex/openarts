frequently asked questions

Can't existing Open Source Licenses be used for Open Artworks?

Existing Open Source licenses deal with literary works, which computer program source code is considered to be for copyright law. An art-specific license will be better suited to artworks.

Doesn't "Fair use" cover this?

No. The OAL gives much broader permission to copy, distribute and use works.

Can I de-license a work?

No. Once you've let the genie out of the bottle you can't put it back in. Open Artworks remain open once they are placed under the OAL.
